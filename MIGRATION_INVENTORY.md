```text
Jousikvartetto

{image 1}

Jousikvartetto on monitaiteinen teos, johon koreografi ja kuvataiteilija Anne Naukkarinen tallentanut perheensä neljän sukupolven muistoja ja tarinoita Ruokolahden Pappilanlahden maisemasta – paikasta, jossa hänen äidinpuoleinen sukunsa on asunut jo yli sadan vuoden ajan.

Jousikvartetto pohjautuu Naukkarisen perheenjäsenistään tekemiin haastatteluihin, joista hän kokosi moniäänisen käsikirjoituksen. Säveltäjä Leevi Räsänen käänsi käsikirjoituksen maisema, muistuma, enne -sävellysteokseksi neljälle jousisoittimelle. Sävellystyössään Räsästä innoittivat mielikuvat paikkaan kietoutuneesta ikiaikaisesta laulusta ja muistojen sumusta. Käsikirjoituksen sisällöt saavat teoksessa äänellisen muodon vuoroin yksittäisen muusikon tulkintoina, vuoroin koko perheen yhteisenä kuorona. Teos kantaesitetään Naukkarisen isoäidin Kerttu Kukkuraisen puutarhassa, josta avautuu näkymä Saimaalle ja Ruokolahden kirkolle.

Teoksessa yhden perheen paikkaan liittyvät muistot punoutuvat äänelliseksi kudelmaksi, joka ulottuu vuosikymmenten yli. Siinä suomalaisen yhteiskunnan laajat kehityskulut, kuten maaseudun rakennemuutos, rinnastuvat arkisiin ja intiimeihin hetkiin — puutarhanhoitoon ja järveen pulahtamiseen — yhtä merkityksellisinä. Henkilökohtaiset muistot muuttuvat perhepiirissä jaetuiksi myyteiksi, jotka väistämättä muovaavat tapoja, joilla ympärillä loputtomasti muuttuva maisema koetaan.

Sävellyksen maisema, muistuma, enne lisäksi teokseen kuuluu pienimuotoinen julkaisu, joka sisältää teoksen alkuperäisen käsikirjoituksen sekä imatralaislähtöisen kuvataiteilija Saimi Suikkasen maisemapiirroksia. String Quartet is an interdisciplinary work by choreographer and visual artist Anne Naukkarinen, in which she captures four generations of her family’s memories and stories from the landscape of Pappilanlahti in Ruokolahti—a place where her maternal lineage has lived for over a hundred years.

The work is based on interviews Naukkarinen conducted with her family members, from which she compiled a multi-voiced script. Composer Leevi Räsänen translated the script into a composition for four string instruments titled maisema, muistuma, enne. In his composition, Räsänen drew inspiration from the imagined presence of an ancient song woven into the landscape, and from the mist of memories. The contents of the script take on an auditory form in the composition—at times through the interpretations of individual musicians, at others as a shared family chorus. The work will premiere in the garden of Naukkarinen’s grandmother, Kerttu Kukkurainen, overlooking Lake Saimaa and the Ruokolahti church.

In the piece, one family’s memories based on a site are woven into a sonic tapestry that stretches across decades. Broader developments in Finnish society—such as the structural transformation of rural areas—are juxtaposed with everyday, intimate moments like gardening or taking a dip in the lake, each rendered equally meaningful. Personal memories evolve into shared myths within the family, inevitably shaping how the constantly changing surrounding landscape is experienced.

In addition to the composition maisema, muistuma, enne, the work includes a small publication featuring the original script and landscape drawings by visual artist Saimi Suikkanen, originally from Imatra. This continues Suikkanen’s exploration of landscape drawing, begun during her 2024 summer residency at the South Karelia Artists’ Association in Ruokolahti. The publication will be available at the performances.
```

```text
Notes on Wind

2025
Poster

Notes on Wind is based on the exchange of letters between choreographer and visual artist Anne Naukkarinen and visual artist Anatole Chartier from May to June 2025. Notes on Wind is a poetic greeting between two artists living in different countries who have never met each other. Artists who are unknown to each other write about their shared interest – the wind.

The correspondence between the Finnish and French artists was conducted in two different languages, each writing in their own native language. Institut finlandais acted as interpreter for the translations of the poetic messages. The final text is published as a poster designed by graphic designer Lia Pradal (Editions Païen) and on the Institut finlandais website in Finnish, French and English.

The concept of Notes on Wind stems from Naukkarinen’s work A Book of Dances, a collection of choreographies written by seven different artists based in Finland and Sweden, published by Moving in November and TUA (Theatre’s New Base Library). The book explores language as a writing-based approach to choreographic practice. It brings attention to the contradictions, translations, and intimate relationships between dance and language.

Notes on Wind is an artistic project that proposes an alternative to international artist mobility and a responsible way to network in the field of visual art and choreography.

Read Notes on Wind in full on the Institut finlandais website.
```

```text
A Book of Dances

2024
Book
Concept and edited by Anne Naukkarinen
Publishers Moving in November & TUA - Theatre's New Base Library

A Book of Dances is a collection of written choreographies by Finnish-based artists Anne Naukkarinen (myself), Laura Cemin, Mikko Niemistö, and Marika Peura, and Swedish-based artists BamBam Frost, Ofelia Jarl Ortega, and Pontus Pettersson. 

{image 1}

The book explores language as a writing-based approach to choreographic practice. It brings attention to the contradictions, translations, and intimate relationships between dance and language. In A Book of Dances, multiple languages are intertwined with the situated knowledge and histories of the participating artists, each embedded within specific social contexts.

The book’s aim is not to define choreographic practices as such, but to create a space for writing that engages with and embodies diverse perspectives on world-making. Thus, the process of meaning-making becomes situated, relational, and unstable—it shifts constantly.
```

```text
Carried by Invisible Bodies

2022
Performance & Installation
Dance, sculptures, poem, harp music

{image 31} 

The performance Carried by Invisible Bodies is a memory holder. But also something else, like a possibility to re-imagine. The bodies’ movements, words, sculptures, and harp music are echoes of something that was once given. They are traces of a repetition that carries, strains, bores and caresses us. 

Alongside dancing, sculpting, playing the harp and reciting poetry are some of the oldest forms of art making. Carried by Invisible Bodies creates a poetical space where the present moment, personal histories and non-human perspectives of time are weaved together. Materials like clay, wax and hay follow their own processes of growth and formation, while the artists' bodies and their ways of making art are connected to layers of human cultural history. The music by Natalia Castrillón, a poem by Maarit Mustonen, and sculptures by Niina Tervo are gathered together and brought in conversation in Anne Naukkarinen's choreography.
```

```text
hear her ears here is a site-specific performance that we processed during our visit at Travers and the Glyptoteket museum. The starting point of our work was the local Danish language that we do not understand. We transcribed the text for our performance through listening people speaking Danish in Glyptoteket on the 28th of November 2018 – we wrote down what we heard and how we heard it. hear her ears here was 1,5 hours long improvised performance that was based on repetition. She listened people speaking in public spaces and written down what she hears and how she hears it: hils stiine – de baiju omsii – oukei – de ee megafiiert – ikkööhäik ikkööhäik. I approached these words as scores: I gave a body and voice for the material by interpreting the words by voice and movements, creating gestures for each utterance. We worked collaboratively by selecting and editing the texts and developing choreography for the performance. 
```

```text
We worked with the embodiment of language, its emotions and logics, and the possible spaces created by words. We examined, how do meanings emerge and sway? Who is the body who moves and talks? What kind of world language creates? 

The piece was composed and performed in a private apartment in Copenhagen in collaboration with speech and language therapist Alma Manley and curator, artist Karin Hald from Forlaget Gestus, 21.-23.9.2018.
```

```text
Anne Naukkarinen (b. 1987) is a Helsinki-based choreographer and visual artist working at the intersections of performance art, choreography, and visual arts. In her artworks, she explores intimate and complex human experiences—emotions, affects, sensations, and thoughts in motion, always in relation to specific situations and environments. Her methods draw from dance and somatic practices, and writing. 

Naukkarinen has created works for dance, performance, and visual arts contexts, as well as for site-specific locations. Her recent works have been presented at e.g. Moving in November, Moderna Dansteatern – MDT, Mad House Helsinki, Titanik Gallery, SIC Gallery, Kunsthalle Helsinki, and Contemporary Art Space Kutomo. Naukkarinen is currently creating a new site-responsive artwork Jousikvartetto commissioned by curator Riikka Thitz and i dolci.

She holds a Master of Fine Arts from the Academy of Fine Arts, Helsinki (2020), and a Bachelor’s degree in Dance from the Theatre Academy, Helsinki (2015). She also studied at the Inter-University Centre for Dance Berlin (HZT) in 2014.
```
